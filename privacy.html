Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 28, 2026

💧

The short version: Sip collects nothing. There’s no account, no sign-up, no tracking, and no analytics. Everything you log stays on your Mac.

Sip (“the app”, “we”, “us”) is a hydration tracker for macOS. We built Sip to be private by design. This policy explains what happens to your information when you use the app — and the simple answer is that your information never leaves your device.

Information we collect

None. We do not collect, store, transmit, sell, or share any personal information. Sip has no user accounts and no login. We never ask for your name, email, or any identifier.

Your hydration data

The drinks you log, your goal, reminder schedule, and preferences are stored locally on your Mac using Apple’s on-device database (SwiftData) and standard app settings. This data is never uploaded to us or to any third party. If you delete the app, this data is removed with it. You can also export your history to a CSV file or erase all data at any time from Settings → Data & Privacy.

Notifications

If you enable reminders, Sip schedules local notifications on your device through macOS. These are generated entirely on your Mac. No reminder content or schedule is sent to any server.

Purchases

Sip offers an optional “Sip Pro” upgrade through Apple’s In-App Purchase system. All payments are processed by Apple — we never see or receive your payment details. Purchase and subscription handling is governed by Apple’s Privacy Policy.

Third parties & tracking

Sip contains no third-party analytics, no advertising, no tracking SDKs, and makes no network connections of its own. There is nothing to opt out of, because nothing is collected.

Children’s privacy

Sip is safe for all ages and does not collect any data from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy ever changes, we’ll update this page and revise the “Last updated” date above. Since Sip doesn’t collect data, we don’t expect material changes.
